A mega-diverse destination, Peru offers travel destination for all types of traveler. This trip of a lifetime will allow you to
connect to the fullest with the Andean Highlands. Inherited from the Incas this beautiful land is known for its colorful
and vibrant culture that mixes more modern influences with the traditions and customs
dating back to the Inca times.

We will start with Lima, Perus proud capital where you can see the best sights and sample its finest food. Next the Andean Highlands await. You will visit the old Inca Capital, Cusco as well as the picturesque Sacred Valley that connects with the highlight of your trip; Machu
Picchu.

Wilkinson Family  May 2019

Machu Picchu is by far South America most impressive archeological site and one of the 7
new wonders of the world. Built in the 15th century and lost to the jungle, forgotten for hundreds of years. In 1911 US explorer Hiram
Bingham (re)discovered the site. Unique in the world, Machu Picchu does not only impress by the quality of its masonry and architectural
feats but also because of the place chosen by the Inca to take up such a daunting undertaking. 

Peru is so much more than Cusco and Machu Picchu. If you would have more time available, Peru is also home to dozens of other amazing bucket list destinations. The Amazon Rainforest, Lake Titicaca, the Colca Canyon and of course the Nazca Lines. Finally Peru is a Walhalla for foodies. Being able to make use of an unparalleled wealth in local ingredients and innovative cooking practices, in Peru food has taken a new level and is completely merged with its local culture and a righteous source of their culinary chauvinism. 

Lima Arrival
 
On arrival to Lima you will be welcomed at the airport and directly taken to your hotel in the upscale neighborhood of Miraflores or Barranco. Here you can unwind after your flight and get ready for the start of your Peru journey.
Lima City Tour
 
Almost one third of the population this massive city has a lot to show for. Beautiful historic buildings, old pre Inca temples, world class museums, the Pacific Ocean and not to forget the new star of the show; Peruvian gastronomy.
A Tour of Cusco
 
Today you will travel to Cusco where you will
welcomed. In the afternoon we will show you the beautiful city center with its typical architecture as well as impressive Inca sites such as Sacsayhuaman and Qenqo. You can also visit the central market, another of
the many highlights the city has to offer.
Sacred Valley 
 
Today we will depart Cusco and head to the Sacred Valley of the Incas. This will be day full of quaint villages, colorful markets and imposing Inca constructions, topped off with a night in the tranquility of this beautiful and lush valley. 
Machu Picchu
 
Today you will travel to what we consider the highlight of Peru Machu Picchu. A beautiful train journey ends in Aguas Calientes Town where your guide will be waiting for you, ready to provide you all the ins and outs of this remarkable site during your private Machu Picchu visit. 
Huayna Picchu
 
This day you have a free day at Machu Picchu. A lot can be done on this day such as hiking the towering Huayna Picchu Mountain for a birds-eye-view of the site before enjoying the spectacular train journey back to the Sacred Valley and return to Cusco.
Your Itinerary
 
During this weeklong itinerary we will guide you through some of Perus most important destinations. We will travel
from the modern capital Lima to the Inca capital Cusco from where we will make our way to the enigmatic lost city of Machu Picchu for a two day visit.
